Untitled #1

watercolour on paper
650mm x 795mm
2016

description

Someone sits on another’s shoulders. Surrounded by a crowd. Is this the scene of a music concert? The elevated figure wears a jersey similar to a classic football jumper made of wool with an itchy collar. Is she being cheered for her 100th game? Or is this a violent scene? a riot? Within all this possibility, why are her eyes closed and why is she so calm? Her calm inner space co-exists with the outer chaos.

Untitled #2

watercolour on paper
630mm x 785mm
2016

Description

A woman bare breasted sits in a car, her hand out the window. She might have a cigarette between her fingers. One might detect a sexual air. She gazes out into the starry deep blue sky. It is hard to tell whether she is relaxed or feigning a relaxed cool posture. Is this the end of an encounter between her and the shadowed figure lurking behind her? Or the beginning?

Untitled #4

watercolour on paper
705mm x 885mm
2016

Description

This could be the image of two women or maybe just one viewed twice in the same picture plane. From behind she seems well groomed, tall posture wearing a fashionable top that sits on one shoulder. The next figure seems dirty, strained and intense wearing a loose t-shirt. Something mysterious is happening between them. There is a transparency and a transition between the two that is hard to grasp.

Untitled #6

watercolour on paper
690mm x 885mm
2016

Description

This image was generated intuitively. It emerged through a series of observations from nature in various locations around Melbourne, I was taken aback by the vivacity and energy of the final effect. Somehow a picture generates an inward strength from time to time and feels complete. Albeit at the time I could’t work out why this image felt special. Two years later when I looked at the picture I realised that although unaware at the time, the image was actually a representation of a strong visual memory from a time in Darwin when I was working on a Cruise boat. In Darwin harbour, there are a lot of small grey (but once white) boats that are anchored between 50 and 200 metres away from the beach. I was shocked to discover the people actually lived their lives in these small boats. I was gazing out at these boats, the sunset, listening to the chatter of the Mindil Beach Sunset Markets that could be heard from the beach, feeling the trade winds blowing gently over the entire landscape. My uncle (the captain of the cruise boat) caught me slackening off on the job and said, “I can see you on one of those when you’re older”.
